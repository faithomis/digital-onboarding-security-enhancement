Digital Onboarding Security Enhancement
Overview

This repository documents a NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)-aligned governance enhancement model designed to strengthen identity access management, audit traceability, and anomaly detection within a regulated digital account opening and onboarding ecosystem.

As onboarding operations scaled across multiple product lines, identity governance and operational traceability controls were enhanced to mitigate insider risk, protect investor data, and improve regulatory defensibility.

Governance Objectives
Reduce unauthorized workflow modification risk
Strengthen audit defensibility across onboarding systems
Enhance detection of anomalous administrative activity
Align operational controls with NIST CSF
Control Layers Implemented
Identity Governance (Protect – PR.AC)
Role-Based Access Control (IAM segmentation)
Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) enforcement
Least privilege policy enforcement
Audit Traceability (Detect – DE.CM)
Centralized logging via CloudTrail
Log validation and encrypted storage
Anomaly Detection (Detect – DE.AE)
Continuous monitoring via GuardDuty
Business Value
Strengthened investor data protection posture
Improved regulatory and audit readiness
Reduced identity and configuration-related risk exposure
Enhanced operational resilience across onboarding workflows
